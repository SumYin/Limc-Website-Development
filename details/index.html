Permitted Utensils

Allowed

  • The materials that students are allowed to have during the competition are the following:
  • Pen/Pencils
  • Erasers
  • Rulers
  • Highlighters
  • Scratch Paper

Prohibited

  • No calculators or phones and similar electronic devices of any kind are allowed. No questions require the use of a calculator.
  • Smart Watches
  • Mobile Phone

Eligibility

Please note that the competition is open exclusively to 10th-grade (17 years of age and below) students and below, considering various factors such as students' preparations for IB exams and the complexity of creating challenging questions for 11th and 12th graders.

Format

The competition format comprises three rounds: Individual, Team, and a distinct component where individual scores contribute to the team score, determining participants for the final round.

Individual Round

11:15 - 12:20

A round of 20 multiple choice questions. It consists of topics such as geometry, algebra, trigonometry, logic and many more. The total score of the competition is 48 points, with questions ranging from 2 and 3 points.

Team Round

12:40 - 1:40

Team round in which a three-people team would be made by their schools to compete. The questions are composed of 10 multiple choice questions and 10 short-response questions. The score is 56 points, with questions ranging from 2,3, and 4 points.

Final Round

2:45 - 4:00

Final round in which 10 top scorers (depending on the individual and team scores) will compete in a podium with questions to determine the winners. There are two type of questions, normal rapid calculation questions and a unique type of question called Relay questions.

Good to Know

All the answers to the questions are verified with the teachers and many seniors in our schools and it is ensured that the answers are completely accurate and correct. We recognize the ultra-importance of the legitimacy of our contest and we promise to not make any mistakes with the competition itself.


Note that our competition does not require any crazy or extremely specific formula to solve any of the questions. We intend to create conceptually and mathematically challenging questions to encourage deep thinking and analysis rather than questions that can only be solved using an unfamiliar strategy.
